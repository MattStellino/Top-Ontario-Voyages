Royal Ontario Museum (ROM)

Insight into the ROM

The ROM was was first founded in 1914 and started construction in 1910, it is widely considered to be among the top 10 institutes in North America of art and culture. Over 13 million pieces of unique and fascinating artwork is located at the museum which are scattered in over 40 exhibition spaces. This world class institute is located in the heart of Toronto and is praised by the citizens and tourists of the city. The ROM holds artworks and objects that date back to prehistoric times all the way to modern day works, these objects were collected from around the world for us to be able to view them. Daniel Libeskind, John Pearson, and Frank Darling are the three men that put their ideas together to create the museum. This explains why the museum itself could be considered an art piece with its dystopian look, and why it instantly stands out in downtown Toronto. The museum is also a popular school field trip location because of its historical significance.
