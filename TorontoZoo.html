Toronto Zoo

The Toronto Zoo is known as the biggest one in Canada as it houses around 5,000 different animals and spans out about 710 acres. Metropolitan Council adopted the Master Plan for the Zoo in 1969 and provided $22 million for development. In addition to the animals coming from the Riverdale Zoo, $6 million had also been contributed by local schools and other agencies for acquiring the animals and plants. The Toronto Zoo, formerly known as the Metropolitan Toronto Zoo, began construction mostly in fall of 1970, but then on August 15, 1974, it opened its gates to the general public. The wild animals were all relocated from Riverdale with the creation of the Zoo, and the area is now home to a pioneer farm.

The zoo is split up into 7 different parts and they are:

  1. Canadian Domain
  2. Tundra Trek
  3. Indo-Malaya
  4. Australasia
  5. Africa
  6. Americas
  7. Eurasia

The Toronto Zoo has more at 535 Staff working year long and more then 1.3 million people vist the zoo each year. The Toronto Zoo is overseen by a Board of Management on behalf of the City and is the property of the City of Toronto. The Board is made up of five members chosen by Toronto City Council and four representatives from the Zoological Society of Toronto.
