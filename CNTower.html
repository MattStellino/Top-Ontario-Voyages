CN TOWER

Currently, the CN Tower serves as Toronto's main hub for communications, providing service to more than 16 Canadian television and FM radio stations. The railway corporation that came up with the concept for the tower's construction is known by the initials CN, which stand for "Canadian National." The CN Tower is one of Toronto’s most popular tourist attractions and there are some famous activities for you to do, like the edgewalk, the glass floor, the 360 Restaurant, and more.


Fun Facts/History

  • Construction started February 6, 1973 and ended on April 2, 1975.

  • Public opening on June 26, 1976.

  • Building the CN Tower costed $63 million and spanned over 40 months.

  • One full round at the 360 Restaurant takes 72 minutes.

  • It took over 1,000 workers to build the CN Tower

  • The CN Tower gets hit by lighting more than 75 times a year.

  • The American Society of Civil Engineers named the CN Tower one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World in 1995.

  • The Tower weighs 117,910 metric tonnes.

  • Based on the event or season, the sophisticated LED lighting system in the Tower changes colour.

  • The CN Tower is the highest free-standing building in the western hemisphere at 553 metres.
