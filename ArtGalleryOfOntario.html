Art Gallery of Ontario

 Art Gallery of Ontario

The Art Gallery of Ontario was established in 1900 by a group of individual people as the Art Museum of Toronto, and is currently one of the biggest art galleries in North America, housing approximately 90,000 paintings in a 583,000 square foot space. Usually general populace can examine well over 4,000 pieces from the inventory. To this day it is worth $315 million in today's money. A combination of public and private funds are used to support the National Gallery. It would take around 2 hours to go through everything in the building, but if you really want to enjoy the exprenince then 3 or 4 hours would do.

Who Designed The Building?

The transformation of the AGO was made possible by the unprecedented financial and artistic contributions of Ken Thomson, the dedication of numerous other kind donors, the crucial assistance of the provincial and federal governments, and a ground-breaking addition designed by the renowned architect Frank Gehry.
